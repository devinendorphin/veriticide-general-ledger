Per-ﬁrm translation

For a single company with $10B revenue, the implied exposure:

~$500M/yr to occupational fraud (ACFE 5% heuristic), plus 4–10%

The structural insight for the board

The most expensive part isn't the theft — it's the veriﬁcation tax.
Every audit, every KYC check, every contract clause, every security
guard, every missile is spend that produces nothing; it merely
restores the transactional baseline that good faith would provide

for free. Economists since Coase and Williamson have known this:

transaction costs are mostly trust costs. High-trust economies get

a structural margin advantage that no operational excellence
program can replicate. Trust functions as capital — it compounds, it
lowers the cost of every transaction it touches, and every point of it

recovered drops straight to margin.

The interpersonal layer feeds the whole stack, which is why I priced

it: childhood betrayal becomes adult health costs, absenteeism, and

reduced lifetime earnings — "abstract" injuries that show up as

physical healthcare claims and productivity loss on someone's
