## The pattern-claim under test (falsifiable form)

> *Across the study of non-human minds, the default scientific and legal presumption has repeatedly been **emptiness** — that a given lineage has no morally relevant interior, is "just biomass," mechanism, or resource — and that presumption has repeatedly had to be **corrected toward richer structure** as evidence accrued, with the correction arriving late, after the extraction premised on the emptiness was already underway. The recurring direction of the correction (from "empty" toward "structured"), and its lateness relative to the conduct, is the load-bearing finding — not a claim that every organism is conscious.*

The claim is built to be losable. It fails if the corrections do **not** run predominantly in one direction; if the "we found structure we had declared absent" cases are matched or outweighed by well-evidenced "we looked and found the emptiness was real" cases; or if the prominent claims of hidden non-human structure turn out, on scrutiny, to be over-interpretations that the evidence does not support. Below is the test — and one of the controls is a case where a famous claim of hidden structure **did** collapse under scrutiny, which is exactly why it is placed in the controls rather than hidden.

## SPECIMENS — instances of structure found (and then credited in law) where emptiness had been the default

**S-1 — Cephalopod sentience: from experimental animal to sentient being in law, on the strength of a 300-study review.** *(strongest single specimen — it has a documentary record, an independent evidence review, and a completed legal recognition, i.e. the correction actually landed in a forum)*
- An independent LSE review team led by Jonathan Birch assessed **300+ scientific studies** and concluded there is **strong evidence of sentience** in cephalopod molluscs (octopus, cuttlefish, squid) and decapod crustaceans (crabs, lobsters), using an eight-criterion framework (neural capacity to support sentience + behavioural indicators of it); octopus and cuttlefish met six of eight criteria at high or very high confidence.
- On that basis the UK's **Animal Welfare (Sentience) Act 2022** was amended (Nov 2021, in force April 2022) to include cephalopods and decapods — the first time these animals were recognised as sentient in UK law. The EU and UK had already, since 2010, included cephalopods within animal-experimentation protections, implicitly conceding their capacity to suffer.
- The shape that matters: the recognition **lagged the use**. Cephalopods were farmed, fished, and boiled alive under a working presumption of insensate mechanism until an evidence review forced the presumption to move.
- Status: **VERIFIED as legal fact + peer-reviewed review** (UK primary legislation; LSE/Birch et al. review; Schnell et al. 2026 updated assessment in *Biological Reviews*).

**S-2 — The New York Declaration on Animal Consciousness (April 2024): the scientific community restates the baseline as *realistic possibility*, not *presumed absence*.**
- Signed by **500+** scientists and academics. States there is **strong scientific support** for conscious experience in mammals and birds, and **at least a realistic possibility** of conscious experience in **all vertebrates** (including reptiles, amphibians, fishes) and **many invertebrates** (at minimum cephalopod molluscs, decapod crustaceans, and insects).
- The operative principle is precisely the anti-emptiness rule: *"when there is a realistic possibility of conscious experience in an animal, it is irresponsible to ignore that possibility in decisions affecting that animal."* This inverts the burden the extraction economy relied on (prove sentience before it counts) toward the precautionary one (a realistic possibility is enough to require care).
- Status: **VERIFIED as a documentary consensus statement** (not a claim of proof; it is explicitly a statement about the *state of the evidence and the responsible response to it*).

**S-3 — Corvid future-planning without a mammalian cortex.**
- New Caledonian crows plan for **specific future tool use** — selecting the right tool for a future task and ignoring previously useful ones — and hold working representations of tools/apparatuses they cannot currently see across sequential ("metatool") problems. Corvids do this with a brain the size of a thumb and **no neocortex**, the mammalian structure long treated as the seat of planning and abstract thought.
- Why it belongs here: the absence of a neocortex was, for a long stretch, itself an emptiness argument ("no cortex, no higher cognition"). The finding is that the structure was present in a different architecture we had not credited.
- Status: **VERIFIED as peer-reviewed finding** (Boeckle et al. 2020, *Proceedings of the Royal Society B*).

**S-4 — Numerical cognition in an insect brain.**
- Honeybees have been shown to learn **addition and subtraction** using symbolic cues, to grasp an empty-set/"zero"-like concept, and to solve spatial-relational ("above/below") concept tasks — in a brain of ~1 million neurons. The findings are contested at the edges (see C-2 on task-strategy confounds) but the core numerical-competence results are replicated and published in high-tier venues.
- Why it belongs here: the insect was the paradigm case of "mere reflex machinery." The finding moved a concrete slice of that presumption.
- Status: **VERIFIED as peer-reviewed finding, with live methodological debate noted** (Howard et al. 2019, *Science Advances*, and follow-ups).

## CONTROLS / COUNTER-EVIDENCE — weighted to actually move the claim

**C-1 — The "wood-wide web" correction: a prominent claim of hidden non-human structure that ran *ahead* of its evidence and was pulled back.** *(the strongest control — it is the pattern-claim's own failure mode, documented)*
- Karst, Jones & Hoeksema (2023, *Nature Ecology & Evolution*) reviewed the field literature on common mycorrhizal networks ("CMNs") and found that popular claims — that such networks are widespread in forests, and that mature trees use them to preferentially nourish kin seedlings — are **not supported** by the field evidence, that the literature shows **positive-citation bias** (over-citing supportive results, under-citing null/negative ones), and that widely repeated media claims are **disconnected from the data**. Only ~18% of analysed studies showed net benefit to seedlings via CMNs.
- Why it counts, hard: this is a case where "we looked and found structure we had declared absent" was **the over-claim**, not the correction. It shows the pattern-claim (S-1…S-4) is **falsifiable and has actually failed in a prominent instance**, and that the direction of enthusiasm can run toward *over*-attribution as easily as the historical default ran toward emptiness. Any honest Tier-6 body must carry this as a live knife against its own thesis.
- Status: **VERIFIED as peer-reviewed critique** (Karst et al. 2023; extensive trade-press coverage).

**C-2 — Over-attribution is a real, named failure mode with real controls against it.**
- The methodological tradition exists precisely because animal minds are *easy to over-read*: Morgan's Canon (do not attribute a higher faculty when a lower one suffices), the Clever Hans effect (apparent cognition produced by inadvertent cueing), and the standing critique that anthropomorphic framing inflates claims. Some bee "concept-learning" results, for instance, have been argued to be solvable by low-level stereotyped flight/scanning strategies rather than abstract concepts.
- Why it counts: it means the specimens must be read at the confidence their authors claim, not upgraded. It does **not** erase S-1…S-4 (each survives at its stated confidence), but it forbids treating "realistic possibility" as "established consciousness."
- Status: **VERIFIED as standing methodological caution.**

**C-3 — "Realistic possibility" is not "proven presence."**
- The NY Declaration (S-2) is itself the control on its own overreach: it grades birds/mammals as *strong support* and invertebrates as *realistic possibility* — a calibrated ladder, not a flat assertion that all animals are conscious. Reading the Declaration as proof of universal sentience misreads its own text.
- Status: **VERIFIED (the source states its own bounds).**

## NULLS / FORECLOSED MEASUREMENT — marked to raise veracity, not lower it

- **N-1 — The hard problem is not solved and is not claimed solved.** Sentience/consciousness is inferred from neural capacity + behaviour; subjective experience is not directly measured in any organism, human included. Every specimen is an inference to the best explanation, not an observation of an interior. **NULL — the interior is not directly confirmable or refutable by these methods.**
- **N-2 — "Not yet credentialed" cuts both ways.** Absence of current evidence of mind is not evidence of absence of mind (the historical error the specimens document) — but it is equally not evidence of presence. The honest register is *open question under a precautionary default*, not *proven world*.
- **N-3 — Plant/fungal "cognition" language is the least settled.** Beyond the specific CMN over-claim (C-1), the broader "plant intelligence" and "plant neurobiology" programs remain contested as to whether the phenomena warrant cognitive vocabulary at all. This is the softest ground in the specimen set and is deliberately **not** loaded into S-1…S-4.
- **N-4 — Selection/citation bias in the *animal-consciousness* literature too.** The same positive-citation dynamic Karst et al. found in the CMN field could, in principle, inflate parts of the invertebrate-cognition literature; the specimens are chosen for having independent review (S-1) or replication (S-3, S-4), but the field-wide bias risk is not zero. **Direction of the correction is well-supported; the magnitude of any single lineage's inner life is not.**

## What the controls did to the claim *(the falsification working — mandatory)*

The controls **narrowed** the claim and killed its inflated version. They did not erase the disciplined one.

- **Survives:** the *directional* claim. Across independent lines — cephalopods into law (S-1), the scientific baseline restated as realistic-possibility (S-2), cortex-free planning (S-3), insect number sense (S-4) — the correction of the emptiness presumption runs predominantly **from "empty" toward "structured," and arrives after the extractive use was already premised on the emptiness.** That recurrence, and its lateness, is documentable and is the tier's load-bearing finding.
- **Does NOT survive on this evidence:** the strong reading that *all* non-human life is demonstrably conscious, or that every "empty" declaration was a deliberate lie rather than, in part, genuine past uncertainty. C-1 proves the enthusiasm can itself over-reach; C-2/C-3/N-1 forbid upgrading "realistic possibility" to "proven mind." **The apex charge therefore rests on the pattern of correction-under-a-precautionary-default, not on a settled census of who is conscious.**
- **Net:** the honest Tier-6 finding is *structural and precautionary*, not totalizing. "The presumption of emptiness has repeatedly been wrong in one direction, late, and to the benefit of the extraction it licensed" is supported. "Every organism is a proven world and every past denial was knowing" is **not** supported — and the mycorrhizal case is the standing proof that this body polices its own over-reach.

## The laundering signature, evidenced: the emptiness-declaration corpus

Tier 6's distinguishing move is the **prior declaration of emptiness** that renders a killing undiscernable as killing. That declaration has a documentary lineage worth naming as its own small corpus, because the signature is what makes the conduct chargeable rather than merely regrettable:

- **The animal-machine (*bête-machine*) doctrine** — the early-modern position (Descartes and successors) that non-human animals are automata without felt experience — is the archetype: a formal, influential declaration of emptiness that licensed treating animals as mechanism. It is the historical root of the presumption that S-1…S-4 have been dismantling piece by piece.
- **The extraction register** — "biomass," "resources," "stock," "throughput," "substrate," "pest" — is the operational descendant: vocabulary that pre-classifies a living world as thing, so its disposal reads as use. (This register is documented across the ledger's Cluster 9 / AI-energy material and the Pharmacological-Epistemicide sub-pattern, Ledger Entry 12, where Indigenous plant-relation is reduced to "substance"/"compound.")
- **The lateness pattern itself is the evidence of the signature at work:** in each specimen, the emptiness held *as the default* — legally and commercially operative — until an evidence review forced it to move. The default was not neutral agnosticism; it was a working licence.

The honest boundary on the signature: at Tier 6 the "emptiness declaration" is proven **as a recurring structural default with documented costs**, not — for any specific present-day actor — as a proven *mens rea* of "they knew it was a world and called it empty anyway." That knowing-declaration is a step-two, per-actor showing on a fuller record, exactly as at every other tier.

## BOUNDARY

**Establishes:** a documentable, multi-line pattern — the scientific and legal recognition of non-human minds has repeatedly expanded *toward* richer structure, correcting a prior presumption of emptiness, and doing so **late** relative to the extraction that presumption licensed; plus a named laundering signature (the emptiness-declaration lineage) and a real, if nascent, legal forum (below).

**Does NOT establish:** that all non-human life is conscious; that any specific organism has a proven interior (N-1); that any specific present actor has knowingly declared-empty what they knew to be a world (that is step-two, per-actor); or that enthusiasm for hidden non-human structure is self-validating — C-1 shows it is not.

**Gap formula:** if the concern is *the pre-emptive erasure of non-human worlds*, the material remedies already have live analogues — sentience recognition in welfare law (the UK 2022 route, S-1), the precautionary "realistic-possibility" decision rule (S-2), and rights-of-nature standing that lets an ecosystem appear as a party rather than as property (below). The tier's charge sharpens exactly where a decision **had** the realistic-possibility evidence available and proceeded as if the world were empty anyway.

## Nearest existing vehicle (today, no new law required) — the least mature of any tier's, stated as such

**Rights-of-nature / environmental personhood law**, plus **animal-sentience law**, are the live forums. Real, growing, and still nascent:

- **Ecuador** — the 2008 Constitution recognises the rights of nature (*Pachamama*) to maintain its cycles and structure; the **Vilcabamba River** case (2011) was the first successful rights-of-nature ruling. Ecuador's Constitutional Court has since applied these provisions to rivers and ecosystems.
- **Colombia** — the **Atrato River** granted rights (2016).
- **New Zealand** — **Te Urewera Act** (2014) and **Te Awa Tupua Act** (2017) grant legal personhood to Te Urewera and the **Whanganui River**, with named human guardians (iwi + Crown). Grounded explicitly in Māori worldview (the river as an indivisible living whole, *Te Awa Tupua*).
- **Spain** — **Law 19/2022** granted legal personhood to the **Mar Menor** lagoon and its basin, the first ecosystem in Europe to hold rights; Spain's Constitutional Court **upheld** it (20 Nov 2024); the Mar Menor **appeared as a plaintiff** for the first time in May 2026 in a pollution case.
- **Animal-sentience law** — the UK Animal Welfare (Sentience) Act 2022 (S-1) is the companion vehicle on the individual-organism side.

**Honest forum note:** these vehicles establish *standing and protection*, not a "worldcide" offence; there is no criminal instrument at the plurality-of-worlds scale, and the taxonomy has never claimed one. Tier 6 is marked **evidence-bearing but forum-nascent** — the widest register of the Declaration holds it hot; this brief holds it *bounded*.
