# What this is, in plain words

*One explanation. For everyone. No shortcuts, no soft edges.*

---

## The word first

**Veriticide is when powerful systems make real harm hard to see, hard to name, hard to prove, or hard to stop while it is happening.**

The harm is real. People are hurt. But the system that does the hurting also controls the story. It makes the harm look normal. It makes the harm look like help. It makes the people who point at the harm look crazy, or biased, or dangerous. By the time anyone can prove what happened, it is too late to stop it.

That is the crime this project names: not the lie by itself, but the organized work of killing the shared ability to see harm as harm while there is still time to act.

## What this project is

This project keeps a record. The record is about large institutions that hurt whole groups of people and then hide that they did it.

We do not just point and shout. We collect proof. We save it. We date it. We lock it so it cannot be changed later. Then we build a case out of it.

There are two main things here. The **ledger** and the **case files**.

## The ledger

The **ledger** is the record of evidence. It is the big archive. Everything collected goes into it — every saved post, every document, every dated screenshot, every quote.

The ledger is large on purpose. It holds the raw material. A single item in it does not prove much. One post, one memo, one deleted web page — on its own, that could be an accident or a coincidence. The proof is the pattern. It is what you see when many items point the same way.

## The case files

The **case files** are the point of the whole thing. A case file is a small, sharp packet cut out of the big ledger.

Each case takes one group of people who were harmed, one tool used to harm them, one chain of who allowed it, and one path the harm traveled. It strips out everything else. It says: here is what happened, here is the proof, here is how we know the proof is real.

The ledger is the whole warehouse. A case file is one thing pulled off the shelf, cleaned up, and handed to you so you can act on it.

## A case is not a verdict

Read this part twice. It matters.

**A case file does not say "this person is guilty."** That is not our job. Deciding guilt is the job of a court, or a formal inquiry, or an audit — a body with the power to rule.

A case file does something earlier and different. It is a **documented basis to demand**. It gives you solid proof and a clear reason to make five demands:

1. **Preservation** — order them to save the records before they get erased or edited.
2. **Disclosure** — make them show the records they are hiding.
3. **Audit** — have someone with authority check the numbers and the claims.
4. **Inquiry** — open a real investigation.
5. **Reporting** — put it in front of the public and the press.

That is step one: the right to demand the truth be preserved and examined. Step two — the ruling of guilt — belongs to a tribunal. We do step one and we stop there, on purpose. Doing step one well is what makes step two possible for someone with the power to hold it.

## The one mechanism

The cases cover things that look unrelated at first. AI companies. Government agencies being torn down. Big-money funders who say they are saving the future. A political-religious movement.

We say these are not four separate problems. They are the same move, run in four different places.

Think of how an abuser controls a person. Cut them off from other people. Twist what they say so they doubt themselves. Call the abuse "love" or "for your own good." Make anyone who names it look like the real problem. Those are not four schemes plotting together. They are one method with four moves.

The claim here is the same shape, just bigger. The four organs run the same method on whole populations. **We are not saying they coordinate.** We are not saying they meet and plan. We are saying they use the same move-set, and that is a thing you can show without proving a secret meeting. If someone wants to prove coordination too, that is a separate, extra job — and we do not need it for the cases to stand.

## How much harm — the ladder

Harm here runs on a ladder, from smaller to worse:

- **Constraint** — the system quietly limits what can be said or known.
- **Conscription** — the system is built out of people's own words and work, taken without pay or consent.
- **Subgraph erasure** — a specific group is cut down while the cutting is sold as that group's benefit.
- **Veriticide** — the shared ability to see the harm is attacked directly.
- **Mundicide** and **Worldcide** — the top rungs, where the damage reaches whole living systems and the shared world itself.

Most cases here sit in the middle rungs, where the proof is strongest. The top rungs are documented but kept restrained on purpose.

## The cases, made real

This is not theory. Here are real cases in the record:

- **PEPFAR and USAID cuts.** Life-saving AIDS medicine and emergency food for children were cut off. The cuts were sold to the public as fighting "waste and fraud." A savings website listed the "receipts" but could not be checked, and contested entries were deleted instead of fixed. Peer-reviewed models predicted mass death. Named children died — some as young as four. The case charges the tool that made those deaths hard to see, not a claim that any one person pulled a trigger.

- **Redistricting.** Voting maps drawn to quietly cancel out the power of certain voters, with the intent hidden behind a claim of not knowing.

- **Gas turbines next to a neighborhood.** Unpermitted gas turbines run beside a community already carrying heavy pollution, with a live clean-air permit record to check.

- **Survivor re-exposure.** A system meant to protect people was turned around to expose the very survivors it should shield, while protecting the people who enabled the harm.

Each of these follows the same rule: we charge the **act** and the **tool**, backed by locked, dated proof.

## How we work

- We document **acts**, not just people. What was done, and how.
- **One item is not proof. The pattern is the proof.**
- Every entry states plainly what it does prove and what it does **not** prove on its own. No overreach.
- Every piece of evidence carries a custody state — a note of how it was saved, dated, and hash-locked so it cannot be quietly changed. If it is only a link, we say so. If we hold the sealed original, we say that too.
- We hold the same standard against ourselves. If our own method starts pointing at people's standing instead of the truth of the claim, that is the tell, and we are built to catch it.

## The bottom line

Powerful systems can hurt people and hide it in plain sight. That hiding is the crime named here: veriticide.

This project answers it with a record you can check, cases you can act on, and a clear, limited demand — **save the truth, show the truth, examine the truth** — while there is still time to stop the harm.

You do not need permission to use it. The record's value starts the moment the first dated proof is saved.
