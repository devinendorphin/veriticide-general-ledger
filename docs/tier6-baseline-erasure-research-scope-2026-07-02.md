# RESEARCH SCOPE — making the "baseline-erasure" far leg measurable (Tier 6) — 2026-07-02

*Companion to `docs/tier6-worldcide-evidentiary-brief-2026-07-02.md`, sub-mechanism **temporal emptiness-declaration / baseline erasure** (shifting baseline syndrome, Pauly 1995). That sub-mechanism was filed with an explicitly-conceded **untestable far leg**: the claim that any specific present forest baseline is a depleted shadow of a pre-industrial / pre-agricultural richness. This document scopes the path that could convert the far leg from "conceded untestable" toward "measurable" — the Tier-6 analogue of what the GDA assay did for Tiers 1–2 and the pattern brief did for Tier 5. It is a **measurability scope (step-zero)**, not executed research and not a pre-registration ready to run; it identifies the modalities, what each can and cannot establish, the failure modes, and the pre-committed criteria under which a result would count as support or refutation.*

*Custody: web-retrieved 2026-07-02, URLs per item, LOCATOR-VERIFIED. X-1 discipline: each modality is described at the resolution the primary literature claims for it, and the boundary between what it measures and what the Tier-6 claim asserts is stated, not blurred.*

---

## The single most important line in this document

**No available modality recovers past network *topology or function*.** Sedimentary DNA, pollen, and the fossil record recover *community composition, richness, and host-vegetation context* — not "were these particular trees wired into a functional resource-sharing web." Therefore the most this research program can do is make a **narrower, genuinely falsifiable** claim testable:

> *Did the mycorrhizal community (composition, richness, ecto-vs-arbuscular balance) systematically change — and specifically diminish or simplify — coincident with the local onset of agricultural and industrial intensification, at the same or comparable sites; and is that change absent from the "natural baseline" against which present forests are judged?*

That claim is the **honest, testable core** of the baseline-erasure mechanism. It does **not** resolve the Simard/Karst functional-network dispute (C-1 in the brief), and any scope that pretends it does is committing the exact over-reach the brief's C-1 control exists to police. Converting the far leg means making *this* claim measurable — not proving the forest was "wood-wide-webby."

---

## The claim under test (falsifiable form, pre-committed)

> *At sites with continuous sedimentary archives spanning the pre-agricultural → agricultural → industrial transition, mycorrhizal-fungal richness and/or the ectomycorrhizal share declines in a step that tracks the timing of documented local human impact (deforestation, tillage, nitrogen loading), rather than being flat, rising, or shifting at times unrelated to human impact. The decline, where found, is not represented in modern "reference" or "old-growth" baselines used to judge present forests.*

**How it loses:** if multi-site sedaDNA shows mycorrhizal richness flat or increasing across the human-impact transition; if the shifts cluster at climatic rather than anthropogenic boundaries; or if the "modern baseline" already encodes the pre-impact state (no forgetting occurred). Any of these refutes the near-far bridge and the far leg stays untestable-or-false, not rescued.

|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Sedimentary ancient DNA (sedaDNA) metabarcoding** | Fungal (incl. mycorrhizal) taxon composition & relative richness through time, from lake/bog cores | **The core test.** Whether mycorrhizal community composition/richness shifted, when, and whether the timing coincides with local human-impact proxies in the same core. ## Nulls / failure modes — marked to raise veracity, not lower it

- **N-1 — Taphonomic and detection bias in sedaDNA.** DNA degrades, leaches, and preserves differentially by taxon; metabarcoding is compositional (relative, not absolute abundance); primer/database bias under-detects some Glomeromycota. A measured "decline" could be a preservation artefact. **Control required:** taxonomically-matched preservation controls and multi-proxy (pollen/charcoal) corroboration in the same core.
- **N-2 — Correlation ≠ the functional claim.** Even a clean richness decline coincident with human impact supports *community simplification*, not loss of a *functional web*. The document's top line is this null made structural: the near-far bridge stops at composition.
- **N-3 — Chronosequences beg the question here.** Space-for-time is valid only when sites share a trajectory (Walker 2010); baseline-shift is exactly the claim that the trajectory itself moved. Using chronosequences to test baseline-shift is close to circular — hence its "almost nothing" cell above. Included precisely so it is not reached for naively.
- **N-4 — Attribution to *human* impact vs climate.** Late-Holocene fungal turnover has climatic drivers too; disentangling anthropogenic from climatic causation requires the human-impact proxy to be *in the same core* and time-resolved against the fungal signal, not assumed.
- **N-5 — "Forgetting" is a second, separate claim.** That a decline occurred (measurable) is distinct from that present baselines *fail to encode it* (a claim about current reference datasets and management targets). The second needs its own check: compare reconstructed pre-impact community to the composition assumed in present "reference/old-growth" baselines.

## Conversion criteria — what moves the far leg, pre-committed

The far leg's status changes as follows (declared before any result, per the framework's own asymmetry/reflexivity discipline):

- **→ MEASURABLE / SUPPORTED (near-far bridge holds):** multi-site sedaDNA shows mycorrhizal richness or ecto-share declining in a step time-locked to in-core human-impact proxies (not climate boundaries), corroborated by pollen/charcoal, **and** the reconstructed pre-impact community differs from the modern reference baseline (N-5). The *narrow* claim — anthropogenic mycorrhizal-community simplification, forgotten by current baselines — is then evidence-bearing, and the temporal-emptiness sub-mechanism graduates from "conceded untestable far leg" to "measured near-far bridge, functional claim still open."
- **→ MEASURABLE / REFUTED:** richness flat/rising across the transition, or shifts clustering at climatic boundaries, or modern baseline already encodes the pre-impact state. The far leg is then not merely untestable but *tested and unsupported*; the brief must record it as such.
- **Remains UNTESTABLE (functional layer):** in all cases, "the forest was a functional resource-sharing web before industrialization" stays out of reach on these modalities. This is not a gap to be closed by more of the same data; it is a category boundary. The scope's success is defined as making the *composition/forgetting* claim decidable, **not** as resolving the functional dispute.

---

## Scope boundary

**This document establishes:** that the baseline-erasure far leg has a **real measurability path** through existing paleoecological modalities (principally sedaDNA + pollen/charcoal corroboration), with pre-committed support/refute criteria — so it is no longer accurately described as flatly "untestable." It is *untested*, with a defined test.

**This document does NOT establish:** any result (no data are analysed here); that the functional "wood-wide web" claim is testable (it is not, on these modalities); or that a single-site finding would suffice (multi-site, multi-proxy, human-impact-in-core is the minimum bar). It commissions nothing and asserts no tribunal-facing claim; it is step-zero measurability scoping in the sense of the Documentation & Standing Protocol.

**Feasibility note (honest):** the strongest near-term move is **re-analysis of already-published fungal sedaDNA cores** that also carry human-impact proxies, screened for mycorrhizal taxa across the agricultural/industrial transition — no new fieldwork, existing datasets. That is the cheapest path from "conceded untestable" to a first decidable result, and the appropriate next step if this scope is taken up.
