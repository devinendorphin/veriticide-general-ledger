## A. The framing

- After exposure was identified, the government said it was **"fixing thousands of documents"** in
  the Epstein-related files that **"may have had victim information,"** and attributed the exposure
  to **"technical or human error."**
- **Thousands of files were taken down** after survivors' names and images were found in them.

## B. Why it is in this case (Move 5 — euphemism / abstraction)

"Technical or human error" is the laundering move: it recodes a **foreseeable, repeated, and
scale** exposure (thousands of documents; multiple releases; assurances given and then breached —
see `victim-info-exposed`) as an **accident** — an isolated, blameless mishap. The scale and
repetition are the tell that strains the framing: a one-off slip is an error; thousands of documents
across releases, after assurances, is a **process failure** whose foreseeability the euphemism
conceals. Paired with the asymmetry (`redaction-asymmetry`), the "error" account must explain why
the error ran toward exposing victims and not toward exposing enablers.
