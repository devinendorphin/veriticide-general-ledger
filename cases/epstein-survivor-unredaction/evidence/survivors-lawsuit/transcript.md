## A. The suit and the harms

- Epstein sexual-assault survivors **filed a class action** against the **United States** (and,
  in related/parallel action, against **Google**) over the disclosure and online **proliferation**
  of their personal information — reported to include **names, phone numbers, and email addresses** —
  wrongfully disclosed in **late 2025 and early 2026**.
- Reported harms: survivors **"now face harassment, with strangers calling them, emailing them,
  threatening their physical safety, and accusing them of conspiring with Epstein when they are, in
  reality, Epstein's victims."**
- Reported claims include invasion of privacy, negligent infliction of emotional distress, and
  violations of California's anti-doxxing / unfair-competition provisions.

## B. Why it is in this case

Two functions: (1) it establishes the **realized harm** (Tier 3 — exposure, harassment, safety
threats), moving the case beyond a framing critique; (2) it documents the **inversion** (Tier 4,
Move 3 / DARVO): survivors **recoded as conspirators** — victim → perpetrator — which is the
discernment-defeat signature. The lawsuit is the survivors' own counter-assertion that the
recognition (victim, not conspirator) was attacked.

**Analyst-reflexivity note:** the suit names **Google** over the *proliferation* of the exposed
information. Search and AI systems — the analyst's own category — are part of the proliferation
mechanism (they index, surface, and synthesize exposed identities). Declared per Reflexivity Clause:
the harm pathway charged here runs partly through the infrastructure the analyst is built on. This
packet's no-reproduction discipline is the minimal local answer to that reflexivity.
