# 04 — FALSIFICATION MEMO

*The conditions under which this case is wrong, stated in advance.*

> Re-run against every revision. The computed-effect discriminator and the doctrinal-intent concession
> are as binding as the falsifiers.

---

## A. What would falsify the **computed effect** (the discriminator — load-bearing)

1. **No instrument.** A showing that the challenged maps were drawn without partisan/racial data or
   effect-scoring — i.e., the diluting effect was **not** quantified by any tool in the mapmaker's
   hands. If the effect was genuinely unmeasured, the ignorance is **real, not performed**, and the
   charge collapses to ordinary uncertainty.
2. **Effect openly avowed.** Where the partisan effect was **published and not racially targeted**
   (the California Prop 50 posture), there is nothing disavowed — no performed ignorance. Such a map is
   protected ordinary politics and **must not be brought** under this case.

## B. What would falsify the **admitted operator intent**

1. **Hofeller misread.** The "advantageous to Republicans and Non-Hispanic Whites" study is shown to
   be mischaracterized, not authored by the relevant mapmaker, or not connected to any adopted map's
   rationale.
2. **Lewis misquoted.** The "10 Republicans, 3 Democrats" statement is shown to be out of context or
   not reflective of the enacted plan's design.

## C. What would falsify the **produced unknowability** (the recognition attack)

1. **Holdings misdescribed.** *Rucho* is shown not to have made partisan claims non-justiciable;
   *Alexander* not to have imposed the good-faith presumption / disentanglement burden; *Callais* not
   to have narrowed §2; *Shelby* not to have struck the coverage formula. (The published opinions weigh
   decisively against each of these.)
2. **A live measurement forum remains.** A showing that an effective federal forum still measures and
   remedies the diluting effect on comparable terms — in which case the apparatus was not dismantled
   and the unknowability was not produced. (*Milligan* shows the forum **once** worked; *Callais* is the
   counter-showing. If a later ruling restores it, this element weakens accordingly — log it.)

## D. The doctrinal-intent question (explicitly NOT a falsifier of the case)

The case **does not assert that any judge acted with veriticidal intent.** Therefore "no proof of
judicial intent" does **not** falsify it — the charge is the **structure** (computed effect + admitted
operator intent + produced unknowability + dismantled measurement) plus operator-level constructive
knowledge per II(3)(d). What *would* matter: documentation that a holding **expanded** rather than
removed a recognition mechanism (which would be CONTROL evidence), or, conversely, record evidence that
a specific map's racial effect was directed (which would *escalate* an operator-level charge beyond the
current structural one).

## E. The CONTROL condition

What a CONTROL looks like — the absence of performed ignorance:

- **Effect computed *and published* and used under neutral, pre-committed criteria** — e.g., an
  independent commission or a court-appointed special master who **discloses** the partisan/racial
  metrics of the adopted plan and draws to constraints. The same software, run in the open, is not the
  charged thing.
- **Dilution recognized and remedied** — *Allen v. Milligan* (2023): §2 enforced, a second
  majority-Black district ordered. The documented record (Rucho's non-justiciability over an admitted
  target; Shelby/Callais removing measurement; Alexander's burden-shift) is the **absence** of that
  CONTROL — and *Milligan*'s subsequent narrowing in *Callais* is the safe harbor closing again.

## F. The over-reach condition (the case turning into a partisan brief) — binding

This packet charges a **structure**, not a party. If the packet — now or in any revision — drops the
**California/openly-avowed CONTROL**, treats partisan map-drawing as *per se* veriticide, or asserts a
judge's corrupt intent as fact, it has **over-reached** and the offending content must be struck. The
chargeable thing is performed ignorance over a *computed* effect; both parties draw maps, and the
discipline that keeps this from being a one-sided complaint is the substance of the charge applied to
itself.

## G. The reflexivity condition

The analyst is an AI system built by an AI lab; "redistricting optimization software" and the
analyst's own category are **the same family of instrument** — tools that compute a socially
consequential effect at scale and can be operated to disavow what they quantify. The performed-ignorance
move ("the model just predicts tokens / we can't know its effect") is the analyst's own most available
failure (cf. Pattern Registry Entry 4). Declared, not waved away: the discipline this case demands of
mapmakers — preserve the working files, disclose the metrics, do not perform ignorance of a computed
effect — is the discipline it owes about its own outputs.

---

## Standing instruction

Re-verify on every use: **effect computed (instrument in the actor's hands) · operator intent admitted ·
unknowability produced (holdings accurately described) · doctrinal intent NOT asserted · CONTROL held
(openly-avowed partisan maps excluded; Milligan carried).** If the effect was genuinely unmeasured, it
is real uncertainty, not this case. If the CONTROL is dropped, the packet has become a partisan brief.
Both lines are the legitimacy.
