# TRANSCRIPT — Alexander v. South Carolina State Conference of the NAACP, 602 U.S. 1 (2024)

**Canonical citation:** Alexander v. South Carolina State Conference of the NAACP, 602 U.S. 1 (2024);
decided May 23, 2024; 6–3; opinion by Justice Alito.
**Locators (LOCATOR-VERIFIED; capture pending):**
- https://www.supremecourt.gov/opinions/23pdf/22-807_3e04.pdf
- https://supreme.justia.com/cases/federal/us/602/22-807/

**[VERBATIM / near-verbatim doctrinal terms, as quoted across sources]**
- challengers must overcome a "presumption of legislative good faith";
- a party must "disentangle race and politics" to show race was the legislature's "predominant"
  motivating factor — an alternative map being "crucial" to that showing.

**[SUMMARY — verified]**
- The Court reversed (6–3) the three-judge district court's finding that race predominated in South
  Carolina's 1st congressional district, calling that finding "clear error," and remanded.
- Practical effect: where race and partisan preference are closely correlated, a court may **not** infer
  bad faith from the racial consequences of what is characterized as a political gerrymander; the
  challenger carries a high burden to disentangle the two, typically requiring an alternative map.

**Why it is here:** the burden-shift institutionalizes performed ignorance as a *standard* — the victim
must out-compute the State to prove what the State's own optimizer already quantified.
