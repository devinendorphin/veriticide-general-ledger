**[VERBATIM — the holding, as quoted across sources]**
"partisan gerrymandering claims present political questions beyond the reach of the federal courts."

**[SUMMARY — verified]**
- The Court held that, while partisan gerrymandering may be "incompatible with democratic principles,"
  federal courts cannot adjudicate such claims because they present **non-justiciable political
  questions**.
- The decision left the admitted, precise partisan intent of North Carolina's map (see `rucho-record`)
  without a federal remedy.

**Why it is here:** this is the keystone of the produced unknowability — an effect computed and admitted
is placed beyond the reach of the courts that would recognize it as harm.
