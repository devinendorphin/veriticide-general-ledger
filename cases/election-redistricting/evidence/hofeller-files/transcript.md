Thomas Hofeller's 2015 study concluded that drawing districts using citizenship data (rather than total

**[SUMMARY — verified across the sources above]**
- Hofeller, the GOP's leading redistricting mapmaker for decades, authored an unpublished 2015 analysis
  studying the partisan/racial effect of adding a citizenship question to the census.
- His files (released by his estranged daughter Stephanie Hofeller, published via Common Cause and
  reported by NPR in 2019–2020) showed he played a key role in the Trump administration's decision to
  add the citizenship question to the 2020 census.
- In 2019 the U.S. Court of Appeals for the Fourth Circuit cited the Hofeller document in remanding on
  the question of whether the administration's stated rationale for the citizenship question was a
  **pretext** for a discriminatory purpose.
