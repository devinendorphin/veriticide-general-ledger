**[SUMMARY — verified; quotations to be fixed to the slip opinion at capture]**
- The Court struck Louisiana's congressional map (SB 8) that had provided two majority-Black districts —
  a map enacted after years of §2 litigation (Robinson v. Landry).
- Reported effect (Campaign Legal Center; SCOTUSblog; CRS LSB11431): the decision **eviscerated /
  sharply narrowed Section 2** of the Voting Rights Act as applied to redistricting and imposed a more
  stringent standard on challengers alleging racial vote dilution; while §2 compliance "can provide a
  compelling interest," the Court found no compelling interest justified the second majority-minority
  district here.

**[REALIZED HARM — web-confirmed cascade; the Tier-3 anchor]**
- Louisiana enacted a new map **eliminating one of its two majority-Black districts** in response to the
  ruling (NBC News, 2026).
- The ruling **cascaded** before the 2026 elections: **Tennessee** drew and passed a map eliminating its
  **sole** majority-minority House district; **Florida** moved to dismantle majority-minority districts.
- These are dated, named removals of representation opportunities — the realized subgraph erasure the
  doctrine renders hard to attribute to *racial* (vs. permissible partisan) dilution.
