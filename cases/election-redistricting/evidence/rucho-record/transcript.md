# TRANSCRIPT — David Lewis "10 Republicans, 3 Democrats" admission

**Canonical sources (LOCATOR-VERIFIED; capture pending):**
- Common Cause, "Background on Common Cause v. Rucho": https://www.commoncause.org/articles/background-on-common-cause-v-rucho/
- Rucho v. Common Cause record / widely reported (e.g. Wikipedia overview): https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rucho_v._Common_Cause

**[VERBATIM — North Carolina Rep. David Lewis, chair of the redistricting committee, 2016]**
"I propose that we draw the maps to give a partisan advantage to 10 Republicans and three Democrats,
because I do not believe it's possible to draw a map with 11 Republicans and two Democrats."

**[SUMMARY]**
- The statement was made during North Carolina's 2016 congressional redistricting process and became
  the record evidence of explicit, quantified partisan intent behind the enacted map.
- This is the operator-level admission that meets the *Rucho* non-justiciability holding (see `rucho`):
  the intended effect was stated to the decimal, then declared beyond the reach of the federal courts.

**Why it is here:** it fixes intent at the operator level — actual, admitted, precise — so the
ignorance that the doctrine later performs cannot be attributed to genuine uncertainty.
