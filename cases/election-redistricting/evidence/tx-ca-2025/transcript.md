**[SUMMARY — verified]**
- **Texas:** lawmakers redrew congressional maps mid-decade at President Trump's urging to net ~5
  Republican-friendly seats. A federal panel, 2–1, found "substantial evidence" that "Texas racially
  gerrymandered the 2025 map."
- **California:** voters approved **Proposition 50**, redrawing maps to net ~5 Democratic seats to
  counter Texas. Reporting notes California "drew districts overwhelmingly for partisan reasons" with
  nothing indicating race "predominantly or excessively" drove the lines.

**Why it is here (the discriminator):** the two cases sit on opposite sides of this case's defining line.
Texas's map is alleged to *disguise* a racial effect as partisanship (performed ignorance); California's
partisan effect was *openly avowed* and not race-predominant — protected ordinary politics under *Rucho*,
and therefore **not** a performed-ignorance specimen. Carrying both is what keeps the charge structural
rather than partisan.
