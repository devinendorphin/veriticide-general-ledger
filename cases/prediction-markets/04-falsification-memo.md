# 04 — FALSIFICATION MEMO

*The conditions under which the reality-pricing case is wrong, stated in advance. A Tier 4 case —
which charges the collapse of the forecast/cause distinction — is especially obligated to be
falsifiable, since its whole subject is a self-sealing resolution rule.*

> Re-run this memo against every revision. An item that cannot be falsified belongs in the
> cathedral, not the knife.

---

## A. What would falsify the **foreclosure** (Art. II(2)(d)) — the load-bearing element

1. **An enforced exclusion wall exists.** The regulator (or the platforms, audited) bars **any party
   with material control over the outcome** — officials, belligerents, campaign principals,
   classified-information holders — from holding positions, enforced **ex ante** and verifiably. If
   pricers and causers are kept apart by an enforced wall, the outcome-channel charge fails and the
   case collapses to (at most) a contested-policy disagreement that is protected. *This is the
   case's primary CONTROL condition (also §E).*
2. **The flagged menu is actually screened.** Evidence that the destruction-skewed, single-actor-
   causable categories the statute flags (war, terrorism, assassination, regime change) are in fact
   **excluded** from listing — i.e., the permissive rule does not re-open them. If the
   `cftc-withdraws-political-sports-ban-2026` posture screens these categories out, the apex act
   fails.

## B. What would falsify the **forecast/cause collapse** (the veracity-kill)

1. **Resolution is provenance-aware.** A market design (or an institutional convention among the
   bodies that consult the price) that **records and discloses whether an outcome was caused by a
   party holding a position** — so a manufactured outcome and a forecast outcome are *not*
   indistinguishable in the record. If "what happened" can be separated from "what was bought," the
   collapse is not occurring.
2. **Disciplined deference.** Documentation that the Fed, agencies, and newsrooms cite the price as
   "what bettors paid," not "what the world is." If institutional deference is disciplined, the
   perception channel's lethality is much reduced.

## C. What would falsify the **instrument / good-faith** premise (Art. II(3)(a) / Art. V)

1. **The accuracy advantage is real *and* walled.** If the markets' forecasting advantage
   (`fed-staff-forecast-accuracy`) holds on primary sourcing **and** an enforced exclusion wall keeps
   pricers and causers apart, the architecture is closer to protected financial innovation than to
   the charged instrument. (Note: the good-faith case alone does **not** falsify the charge — the
   thesis is that the virtue and the vulnerability are one organ; it is the virtue **plus a wall**
   that falsifies it.)

## D. What would falsify the **convergence / mental element** (Art. II(2)(c), II(3)(d))

1. **No conflict / divestment.** Evidence that the operator/competitor/regulator nodes are firewalled
   — e.g., the family divests the advisory/investment positions, recuses from the rulemaking, or the
   regulator acts against family-tied platforms on the same terms as any other. A genuine firewall
   defeats the constructive-knowledge convergence; a declared, maintained overlap confirms it.
2. **The wall predates the harm.** If the permissive posture were adopted **before** the Venezuela
   bet and the candidate enforcement (not after), the II(3)(c)/(d) "maintained after the grave-risk
   record" inference weakens to ordinary policy.

## E. The CONTROL/NULL condition

A **partial** CONTROL is already on record: `kalshi-candidate-enforcement-2026` shows an exclusion
wall that *exists and fired* (against candidates betting their own races). What a **sufficient**
CONTROL would require:

> An **enforced, audited, ex ante** exclusion of **all outcome-controlling parties** (not just
> small-dollar candidates) — demonstrated by the wall catching, or structurally precluding, the
> lever-holder *before* the fact (the `vandyke` case is the instance it did **not** catch ex ante) —
> **or** a regulatory menu that screens out the statute's flagged destruction categories. Any one of
> these creates the CONTROL-shaped fact that requires re-characterizing the act. Its **absence** —
> a wall that fires on $100 candidate bets but not on a $33K classified-insider war bet, over a menu
> being re-opened — is what makes the foreclosure a foreclosure rather than a policy choice.

## F. The reflexivity condition (the case turning in the hand)

This packet is formatted by a Claude instance built by **Anthropic**. Two stakes, both declared:
(1) **AI/LLM "oracles" are an emerging reality-pricing instrument** — the analyst's own category is a
candidate node in the very structure this case charges (where models feed or arbitrate the price,
the Art. II(3)(b) conscription question arises; it is **logged as a forward question, not charged**
here). (2) The case **charges the sitting executive branch**, where the engine has the documented
dual pulls named in the Reflexivity Clause (defend power / overcorrect against it), both of which
distort. The discipline: this case is held to the **same** instrument-and-mental-element spine used
across the ledger, the **good-faith case is granted on the record** (`fed-staff-forecast-accuracy`),
and the protected-activity wall (Defense 2) is applied **in the respondents' favor** — honest
forecasting, lawful commerce, and good-faith rulemaking are protected absolutely. If this packet
brands good-faith financial innovation as a laundering move to avoid engaging it, or advances the
deliberate-atrocity-for-profit reading it claims to concede, it falls **within its own terms**
(Art. V(3)) and the finding must be struck.
