## A. The statement and the actions

- The President posted on Truth Social: **"It is critically important that the CFTC's exclusive
  authority over Prediction Markets is maintained, and that they will thrive."** Related lines:
  *"Under my leadership, we are setting 'rules of the road' that are the Gold Standard for the
  States,"* and *"Other Countries are after this new form of Financial Market, and we want to remain
  at the top."* The post **praised CFTC chairman Michael Selig by name.**
- The administration has **sued states** (reported: Minnesota, New York, Arizona, Wisconsin,
  Connecticut) over policies restricting residents' use of prediction markets — asserting that the
  federal government alone may regulate event-contract platforms.
- The White House's Office of Information and Regulatory Affairs began reviewing a proposed CFTC rule
  on prediction markets, signaling advance toward a broader federal framework (elections, gaming,
  sports).

## B. Why it is in this case

This is the **executive-endorsement / authorization node** (Track B — constructive authorization,
Entry 17). It is what makes the conflict *vertical* rather than incidental: the head of the executive
branch — the branch that *causes* the geopolitical and policy events being priced — publicly directs
that the markets "thrive," praises the regulator deciding their rules, and litigates to clear state-
level obstacles, while his son advises both dominant platforms and his company launches a competitor.
The "benefit reframe" (Move 6) is explicit in the post's own words: *financial innovation* and
*remain at the top*.
