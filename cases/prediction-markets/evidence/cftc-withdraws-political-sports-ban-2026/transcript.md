## A. The facts on the record

- **CFTC chairman Michael Selig** directed staff to **withdraw the 2024 rule proposal** that would
  have **prohibited political and sports-related event contracts**, and to withdraw a 2025 staff
  advisory cautioning registrants about sports-related contracts.
- The Commission withdrew the proposed rules to reconsider them "in light of various forms of state
  regulatory actions and litigation concerning the Commission's exclusive jurisdiction over event
  contract derivatives."
- On **June 10, 2026**, the CFTC — **by the vote of its sole Commissioner** — issued a notice of
  proposed rulemaking establishing a framework for determining **when event contracts involving
  gaming, war, terrorism, assassination, or unlawful activity are contrary to the public interest**
  under the Commodity Exchange Act (CEA). The CEA's enumerated "special categories" still read:
  **unlawful activity, terrorism, assassination, war, and gaming.**
- Net effect: a shift from a proposed *ban* on political/sports contracts toward **permissive rules**
  outlining how platforms can offer contracts on politics, sports, and other real-world events, with
  case-by-case "public interest" limits.

## B. Why it is in this case (the apex act)

This is the **Art. II(2)(d) foreclosure**: the protective wall that would screen the destruction-
skewed, single-actor-causable contract menu (war / assassination / regime change — the very
categories the statute flagged) is being **stood down and replaced with a permissive framework** —
and it is being done by a **single commissioner**, **praised by the President**
(`trump-cftc-thrive-post-2026`), **after** the Venezuela insider bet
(`vandyke-venezuela-insider-bet-2026`) and the Kalshi candidate enforcement
(`kalshi-candidate-enforcement-2026`) put the outcome-channel harm on the record. A permissive
posture toward war- and assassination-adjacent contracts maintained *after* that record is the
case's clearest **mental-element / knowing-the-grave-risk** artifact (Art. II(3)(d)).
