## A. The redesign

- **Paid verification (Twitter Blue / X Premium):** the blue check — formerly a trust/identity
  marker that helped distinguish authentic from inauthentic accounts — was converted into a
  **purchasable** subscription feature carrying amplification/visibility benefits.
- **Creator revenue-sharing / payouts:** X introduced payments to accounts based on engagement,
  **monetizing engagement** directly.
- Trade and analysis coverage describes the combined effect as **redesigning the incentive for
  engagement-farming**: where bot-driven engagement was once a cost the platform absorbed, the
  Blue + payouts structure makes high-volume engagement (including inauthentic/farmed engagement)
  **revenue-aligned** for both the operator and, via subscriptions, the platform.
- Commentary (e.g., a proposed "$1 bot tax" on new accounts) frames the platform's own measures as
  unlikely to fix — and in tension with — the engagement-revenue model.

## B. Why it is in this case

This is the **economic substrate of Habitat Authorization** (Entry 15, Mechanism 1): the owner
need not run a single bot-swarm to be the load-bearing cause of their flourishing if the platform's
own incentives reward the behavior. Selling the legibility signal (the check) and paying out on
engagement are **design decisions** that make the habitat hospitable to manufactured consensus.
They are the affirmative-conduct half of the case, paired with the omission half (foreclosed
measurement, `research-api-foreclosure-2023`).

**What it does not establish (do-not-overclaim):** that any particular subscriber or payout
recipient is inauthentic; only that the incentive structure foreseeably rewards engagement-farming.
