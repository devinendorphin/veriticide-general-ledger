## A. The finding

- A **Cyabra** analysis, conducted in partnership with **GZERO** (GZERO Media, published **October
  31, 2024**), examined **five election-related Musk posts**.
- Results reported:
  - **~20%** of accounts interacting with these posts were **bots**.
  - On **two** additional posts where Musk **opposed the Harris–Walz ticket**, **~40%** of the
    activity came from **inauthentic accounts**.
  - Cyabra found **"coordinated activity between these inauthentic accounts, with two distinct bot
    clusters working in tandem."**
  - The accounts "were responsible for driving a **disproportionately large share of the
    engagement and traffic**" and showed "an **agenda to influence public perception**."

## B. Why it is in this case

This is the **sharpest concrete instance** of the swarm-substitution thesis the operator named:
not the elimination of inauthentic activity after the takeover, but its **persistence and
re-tuning** — here, coordinated bot clusters disproportionately amplifying the **platform owner's
own political posts**. It is the empirical anchor for Entry 15's claim that the habitat produces
manufactured consensus, and it lands that consensus on the owner's content.

**What it does and does not establish (do-not-overclaim):** it establishes a measured prevalence
of coordinated inauthentic amplification on specific Musk posts. It does **not** establish who
operates or pays for the clusters, or that Musk directs them — the direct-authorship/attribution
node remains the case's contested beam (`03-adversarial-check.md`, Defense 1).
