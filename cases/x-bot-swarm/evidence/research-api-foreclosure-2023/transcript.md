## A. The foreclosure

- In **February 2023**, after the ownership/rebrand changes, **free API access essentially ended**.
  New pricing introduced a Basic tier at **$100/month** (later raised to **$200**), an undisclosed
  Enterprise rate, and a **crippled free tier** (on the order of **1,500 tweet reads/month**).
- The change **broke the best-known independent bot detectors.** Botometer's makers at **Indiana
  University's Observatory on Social Media (OSoMe)** **disabled the original tool** after X
  suspended free data access, then revived a limited successor, **Botometer X (February 2024)**.
- The successor's limitations are explicit (OSoMe's own statement): Botometer X runs in
  **archival mode on pre-June-2023 data**, **holds no records for accounts created after May 31,
  2023**, scores "may not reflect recent activity," and — because BotometerLite was trained before
  advanced generative AI — it **cannot detect bots supercharged by AI**.
- Public-interest researchers warned at the time (TechCrunch; Columbia Journalism Review;
  Coalition for Independent Technology Research open letter) that the API changes would "devastate
  public interest research," and longstanding public tools (Botometer, Hoaxy, disaster-response and
  accessibility bots) shut down.

## B. Why it is in this case

This is the **measurement-foreclosure** element — the omission half of the charge, and the reason
the purge claim is unfalsifiable by design. "We defeated the bots" is asserted at the same time as
the **independent instruments that could test the assertion** (affordable firehose/API access for
academic bot detection) are **priced or shut out**. A purge that cannot be independently measured
is a **verdict, not a purge** (Entry 15, Move 2 + Art. II(2)(d)). The AI-detection gap is
load-bearing for the present-tense claim: the tools went archival in the same window that
generative-AI-driven inauthentic activity scaled, so the platform's "it's handled" cannot be
checked from outside on current data.

**The CONTROL/NULL this item defines:** any published, externally-auditable inauthentic-activity
metric the platform exposes — restored affordable research access, or a third-party-verifiable
prevalence figure — would falsify the foreclosure charge in this row (`04-falsification-memo.md`).
