## A. The reporting

- **CHEQ** (cybersecurity firm): invalid traffic from bots on Twitter **increased** in 2023;
  reporting framed Musk's anti-bot efforts as having fallen short and, by some measures, worsened
  the situation.
- **Academic framing** — "Should we agree to disagree about Twitter's bot problem?" (ScienceDirect)
  — treats the prevalence and measurement of bots as an open, contested, and persistent problem
  rather than a solved one.
- **Cybernews**, "Why is Elon Musk's Twitter struggling with bots?" — trade coverage of continued
  bot/scam activity post-takeover.

## B. Why it is in this case

Entry 15's arc requires the fourth step — **no observable cessation** after the self-declared
victory. This item is the persistence corpus: independent reporting that the underlying condition
the 2022 promise named was not eliminated. It is deliberately held at the corpus level (multiple
secondary/academic sources pointing the same direction) rather than as a single decisive metric,
because the decisive metric — an independently auditable platform-wide prevalence — is precisely
what the measurement-foreclosure item (`research-api-foreclosure-2023`) documents as having been
made unavailable.
