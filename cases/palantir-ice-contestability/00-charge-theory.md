# 00 — CHARGE THEORY

*The surveillance-substrate knife, narrowed: one contract (ImmigrationOS), one protected population
(people subject to ICE targeting via fused error-prone data, incl. citizens/LPRs wrongly swept in),
one authorization chain (ICE sole-source → Palantir), one harm pathway (probabilistic targeting →
liberty deprivation with no contestable basis). Charged at the **contestability gap**, intent OPEN,
**Tier 3 — Tier 4 explicitly declined** (recognition is live).*

---

## The charge, in one sentence

**Palantir built and is being paid to operate ICE's deportation-targeting instrument — the
"ImmigrationOS"/ICM/FALCON/ELITE data-fusion stack that aggregates tax, benefits, DMV, passport/visa,
and license-plate data and emits probabilistic "where a removable person likely lives" scores used to
direct apprehension — an instrument whose automated determinations are used to deprive people of
liberty (arrest, detention, removal) while its data is documented to be error-laden and its confidence
scores are, by report, insufficient to meet the constitutional standard they are used to satisfy,
leaving the targeted population no adequate way to see or contest the basis of the determination.**

The chargeable core is the **contestability gap**: not that the software "decides," but that it
manufactures liberty-depriving outputs that the subject cannot inspect, correct, or contest.

## The spine (order of proof)

> Prove the **instrument** is real and operated by the respondent (the contracts; the named systems) ·
> prove the **authorization chain** (ICE sole-source award → Palantir) · prove the **harm pathway**
> (fused error-prone data → probabilistic targeting → arrest/detention/removal) · prove the
> **contestability failure** (the determination's basis is unauditable/uncontestable by the subject;
> confidence scores used beyond what they can bear) · **concede the attribution joint** (Palantir
> builds; ICE decides) and **charge around it** at the contestability level · and **bound it**:
> recognition is live (Congress, courts, press), so Tier 4 is declined.

## The protected interest

The **liberty and due-process viability** of a delineable population: people subject to ICE
apprehension whose targeting is mediated by Palantir's fused-data instrument — including **U.S. citizens
and lawful residents wrongly swept in** (ICE is documented to have detained/removed thousands of U.S.
citizens; at least one is in active litigation). The group is delineated **downstream of the
instrument's demonstrated/risked outputs**, not off any stated target list.

## The instrument (Art. II(3)(a))

A genuine operational system, not a metaphor: a unified data-fusion + analytics + targeting platform
Palantir is contractually paid to build and support. Contrast Boxtown (combustion hardware): here the
instrument is **informational** — which is precisely why the charge is pitched at **contestability**,
the harm proper to an informational instrument, rather than at emissions.

## Tier placement — **bounded**

- **Tier 3 — subgraph/material + liberty harm: MET (pending the per-case error record).** A delineable
  population's liberty is mediated by an error-prone instrument with no adequate contest path.
- **The contestability-gap register** (the cluster's named charge level — the Habermas inversion: a
  system that forecloses the conditions of challenge) **is the heart of this case.**
- **Tier 4 — veriticide proper: DECLINED.** Recognition **functions**: congressional oversight letters
  (Menendez; Goldman/Wyden/Velázquez), ACLU/EFF documentation, NPR reporting, and active litigation all
  name the harm contemporaneously. Charging Tier 4 here is the over-reach this project refuses.

## The respondent — and the hard joint (conceded)

**Respondent: Palantir** — as builder/operator of the instrument, charged at the contestability level.

**The hard joint — attribution — conceded up front:** Palantir's defense is *"we provide the platform;
ICE makes the decisions; we are a neutral tool."* This is the proceduralism/"only data processing"
alibi (flagged in the project's reflexive specimen via IBM–Hollerith). The case **does not** claim
Palantir intends or executes deportations. It charges that **building and operating an instrument that
emits uncontestable liberty-depriving determinations is itself the act**, at the contestability level —
the same way `cases/x-bot-swarm/` concedes its attribution joint and charges around it. The agency's
co-responsibility is **not** thereby reduced; ICE is the wielder. This packet charges the
instrument-maker's distinct contribution, intent open.

## What this does NOT establish

- **Not Tier-4 veriticide** — recognition is live; declined in writing.
- **Not intent to harm**, and **not that Palantir makes targeting decisions** (attribution conceded).
- **Not that data-fusion/analytics is per se wrongful** — **CONTROL:** a system with auditable inputs,
  published error rates, an effective subject-access/correction/appeal path, and use bounded to what
  confidence scores can bear would **not** be this case. The charge is the **contestability gap**, not
  software.
- **Not a verdict** — step one: a basis to demand the contract, the data-source list, the error/audit
  records, the confidence-score methodology, and an effective contest mechanism. Not a tribunal finding.
