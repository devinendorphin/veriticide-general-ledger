# 06 — DENIAL-VS-RECORD TIMELINE

*The Tier 4 chronology: the growth of the documentary record on one side, the denial and its
escalation on the other. The case is the gap between them — each denial issued **after** more of
the record had closed.*

**Types:** **ROLE** Rubio's authority · **REC** record closing (death/warning) · **DENY** denial
act · **CHAL** formal challenge · **ADJ** independent adjudication.

Custody governed by the canonical indexes. Dates via sanctioned search; confirm exact days at

## What the ordering establishes

1. **Every denial post-dates more of the record.** By May 21 the record already held named
   deaths, his ambassador's cable, his agency's memos, the modeling, and two congressional
   letters. The denial is not an early statement on an open question; it is a verdict against a
   record that had been closing for **three and a half months**.
2. **The escalation tracks the closing record.** As the evidence hardened (May 21 → May 28), the
   denial **hardened too** — from "no one has died" to "it's a lie." The act got *stronger* as the
   truth got *clearer*. That inverse relationship is the Tier 4 signature: the machinery intensifies
   in proportion to the size of the thing it must keep unseen.
3. **Role precedes everything.** The Feb 3 appointment sits at the top: every subsequent warning
   was **his agency's**. There is no point on this timeline at which Rubio could not have known.
4. **Non-response is terminal.** The Jun→Oct gap proves the June demand went unanswered; the denial
   was maintained not by rebuttal but by silence, even as the record kept closing (Nov).

## What this timeline does NOT establish

Exact-day precision for `~` rows; personal receipt of any single internal document (the timeline
shows role + sequence, which is what II(3)(d) requires, not the eyeball); and any causal link
between the denial and a death — not charged (the harm is discernment-defeat, Tier 4).
