## Content (search-surfaced)

- "**Over 360,000 people had already died** as a result of not having food and medication in
  the wake of the funding cuts." *(realized-mortality figure entered into the record)*
- Named cases entered into the record:
  - **Peter** — 10, South Sudan. Contracted HIV from his mother at birth; PEPFAR medication
    kept him alive "**until February**," when, without access to medication, he fell severely
    sick and died.
  - **A pregnant woman in a Liberian village** — hemorrhaged in labor; USAID ambulances "stood
    idle, unable to help" for lack of fuel due to funding cuts; she died mid-journey being
    carried ten miles on foot, "**along with her unborn son.**"
- Projections cited from **USAID's own internal memos**: up to 18 million additional malaria
  cases/yr → as many as **166,000 deaths**; **200,000 children paralyzed by polio** annually;
  **a million children** without treatment for severe malnutrition each year; **28,000** new
  cases of Ebola/Marburg annually.
