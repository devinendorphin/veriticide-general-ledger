## Verbatim / near-verbatim content (search-surfaced)

- DOGE states the listed cancellations represent "**a subset of contract, grant, and lease
  cancellations, representing ~30% of total savings.**" *(The self-labeled "subset" + "~30%"
  framing is the on-the-face admission that the published record is non-exhaustive.)*
- **Methodology criticism:** DOGE calculates a contract's value as its **total potential
  (ceiling) value** — likened by federal contracting experts to "**a credit card maximum**"
  rather than realistic planned spending.
- **Errors / deletions (CBS and others):**
  - A "$149 million" NIH contract was **deleted**, while an NIH contract with the same company
    sharing the same contract ID is **capped at just $1.4 million**.
  - An earlier posting claimed "$8 billion" in savings from a canceled contract due to an
    apparent typo, when the actual contract was worth **$8 million**.
  - The Hill: at least **37% of contract cuts not expected to save money**.
